Help: Magnetic Rigidity Calculator

Overview

The Magnetic Rigidity Calculator is a web-based tool that helps users calculate key properties of ion beams in particle accelerators, like cyclotrons. It determines how strong a magnetic field is needed to bend a charged particle’s path, along with other important values like the particle’s speed, momentum, and magnet requirements.

The application was created by JL Conradie with the help of Grok3.

Features

  • Isotope Selection: Choose from 305 isotopes (from H-1 to Am-241) with preloaded masses and atomic numbers.
  • Energy Flexibility: Enter beam energy in MeV (default) or keV using a checkbox.
  • Charge Check: Ensures the ion’s charge is valid (not more than the isotope’s atomic number).
  • Bending Angle Output: Shows the bending angle in both degrees and radians for clarity.
  • Magnet Adjustments: Update current, radius, or pole gap to recalculate magnet properties like field strength and coil turns.
  • Magnetic Field Warning: Alerts if the magnetic field exceeds 2 tesla, which may be hard to achieve in typical cyclotrons.
  • Detailed Results: Displays isotope details, energy, charge, speed, momentum, magnetic rigidity, equivalent proton energy, and magnet characteristics.

Input Parameters

The tool accepts the following inputs:

  • Isotope: Select from a dropdown of 305 isotopes (e.g., C-12, U-238).
  • Energy: Beam energy (a number ≥ 0), in MeV (default) or keV (if the keV checkbox is checked).
  • Energy Unit: Checkbox to switch between MeV (unchecked) and keV (checked).
  • Charge: Number of positive charges on the ion (a whole number > 0, must not exceed the isotope’s atomic number).
  • Bending Angle: Angle the ion’s path is bent, in degrees (a number between 0 and 360, default: 90°).
  • Pole Gap: Distance between magnet poles, in millimeters (a number > 0, default: 50 mm).
  • Radius: Radius of the ion’s curved path, in meters (a number > 0, default: 1 m).
  • Current: Electric current in the magnet’s coils, in amperes (a number > 0, default: 1 A).

Output Parameters

The tool provides the following results:

  • Isotope Information: Shows the isotope’s name, atomic number (Z), mass number (A), neutral mass (in atomic mass units and electron masses), and ion mass (after removing electrons).
  • Energy: The input energy, shown in MeV or keV based on the selected unit.
  • Charge: The ion’s charge, in units of electron charge.
  • Beta: The ion’s speed as a fraction of the speed of light.
  • Gamma: A factor that adjusts for the ion’s energy at high speeds (relativistic effect).
  • Velocity: The ion’s speed in meters per second.
  • Momentum: The ion’s momentum in GeV/c (a unit of momentum).
  • Magnetic Rigidity (Bρ): The magnetic rigidity in tesla-meters (T·m), which determines the magnetic field needed to bend the ion’s path.
  • Equivalent Proton Energy: The energy a proton would need to have the same magnetic rigidity, in MeV.
  • Bending Angle: The input angle, shown in degrees (to 2 decimals) and radians (to 6 decimals, e.g., “90.00°, 1.570796 rad”).
  • Magnetic Field: The required magnetic field strength in tesla (T), with a warning if it’s above 2 T.
  • Magnetomotive Force (MMF): The magnetic force needed, in ampere-turns (A·turns).
  • Number of Turns: The number of coil turns needed for the given current, rounded up to the nearest whole number.

Formulas Used

The calculations use the following constants and simplified formulas, designed to be easy to understand:

Constants

  • Speed of light: 299,792,458 m/s (how fast light travels).
  • Electron charge: 0.000000000000000000160217662 C (charge of one electron).
  • Atomic mass unit to energy: 1 atomic mass unit (u) = 931.49410242 MeV/c² (converts mass to energy).
  • Atomic mass unit to electron mass: 1 u = 1822.888486209 electron masses (mₑ).
  • Electron mass: 0.000548579909 u (mass of one electron).
  • Proton mass: 1.0078252 u (mass of a proton, H-1).
  • Permeability of free space: 0.000001256637061435917 H/m (a magnetic constant for free space).

Ion Mass

  • Purpose: Find the mass of the ion after removing electrons due to its charge.
  • Neutral mass: Taken from the isotope database (in atomic mass units, u).
  • Ion mass: Neutral mass minus (charge × electron mass).
  • Ion mass in energy units: Ion mass (u) × 931.49410242 = mass in MeV.

Energy Adjustment Factor (Gamma)

  • Purpose: Account for relativistic effects when the ion moves fast.
  • Formula: Gamma = (Energy + Ion mass in MeV) / Ion mass in MeV.
  • Note: Energy is converted to MeV (if input in keV, divide by 1000).

Speed Factor (Beta)

  • Purpose: Calculate the ion’s speed as a fraction of the speed of light.
  • Formula: Beta = square root of (1 - (1 / (Gamma × Gamma))).

Velocity

  • Purpose: Find the ion’s actual speed in meters per second.
  • Formula: Velocity = Beta × 299,792,458 m/s.

Momentum

  • Purpose: Calculate the ion’s momentum (mass × velocity, adjusted for relativity).
  • Formula: Momentum = Gamma × Ion mass in MeV × Beta (in MeV/c).
  • Convert to SI units: Momentum (MeV/c) × 0.000000160217662 / 299,792,458 = Momentum in kg·m/s.
  • Momentum in GeV/c: Momentum (MeV/c) / 1000.

Magnetic Rigidity (Bρ)

  • Purpose: Determine the strength of the magnetic field needed to bend the ion’s path.
  • Formula: Magnetic rigidity = Momentum (kg·m/s) / (Charge × Electron charge) (in T·m).

Equivalent Proton Energy

  • Purpose: Find the energy a proton would need to have the same magnetic rigidity.
  • Steps:
    • Proton ion mass = Proton mass (1.0078252 u) - Electron mass, then × 931.49410242 to get MeV.
    • Proton momentum = Magnetic rigidity × Electron charge (kg·m/s).
    • Proton momentum in MeV/c = Proton momentum × 299,792,458 / 0.000000160217662.
    • Proton gamma = square root of ((Proton momentum in MeV/c)² + (Proton ion mass in MeV)²) / Proton ion mass in MeV.
    • Proton energy = (Proton gamma - 1) × Proton ion mass in MeV (in MeV).

Magnetic Field

  • Purpose: Calculate the magnetic field strength needed to bend the ion.
  • Formula: Magnetic field = Magnetic rigidity / Radius (in tesla, T).

Magnetic Field Strength (H)

  • Purpose: Find the magnetic field strength in amperes per meter for MMF calculation.
  • Formula: H = Magnetic field / 0.000001256637061435917 (A/m, assuming free space).

Magnetomotive Force (MMF)

  • Purpose: Calculate the magnetic force needed to create the field across the pole gap.
  • Formula: MMF = H × Pole gap (m, convert mm to m by dividing by 1000) (in A·turns).

Number of Turns

  • Purpose: Determine how many coil turns are needed for the given current.
  • Formula: Turns = MMF / Current, rounded up to the next whole number.

Bending Angle

  • Purpose: Show the angle the ion’s path is bent, in degrees and radians.
  • Degrees: Input value, shown with 2 decimal places (e.g., 90.00°).
  • Radians: Degrees × 3.141592653589793 / 180, shown with 6 decimal places.

Usage

  1. Enter Inputs:
    • Select an isotope from the dropdown.
    • Enter the beam energy and check the keV box if using keV (default is MeV).
    • Input the ion charge, bending angle, pole gap, radius, and current.
    • Click Calculate to see results.
  2. View Results:
    • Results show isotope details, energy, charge, speed, momentum, magnetic rigidity, proton energy, bending angle (degrees and radians), magnetic field, MMF, and coil turns.
    • A warning appears if the magnetic field is above 2 T.
  3. Adjust Magnet Parameters:
    • After calculating, adjust current, radius, or pole gap in the provided fields.
    • Click Update Magnet Parameters to update the magnetic field, MMF, and number of turns.

Example

Inputs:

  • Isotope: C-12
  • Energy: 100 MeV (unchecked, i.e., MeV)
  • Charge: 6
  • Bending Angle: 90°
  • Pole Gap: 50 mm
  • Radius: 1 m
  • Current: 1 A

Outputs:

  • Isotope: C-12 (Z = 6, A = 12, Neutral Mass = 12.000000 u, 21874.66 mₑ; Ion Mass = 11.996708 u, 21868.69 mₑ)
  • Energy: 100.000000 MeV
  • Charge: 6 e
  • Beta: 0.132890
  • Gamma: 1.008947
  • Velocity: 39858408.76 m/s
  • Momentum: 0.149160 GeV/c
  • Magnetic Rigidity (Bρ): 0.833333 T·m
  • Equivalent Proton Energy (same Bρ): 1389.519668 MeV
  • Bending Angle: 90.00°, 1.570796 rad
  • Magnetic Field: 0.833333 T
  • Magnetomotive Force (MMF): 33157.30 A·turns
  • Number of Turns (I = 1 A): 33158

Adjust Magnet Parameters:

  • New Current: 2 A
  • New Radius: 2 m
  • New Pole Gap: 100 mm
  • Updated Outputs:
    • Magnetic Field: 0.416667 T
    • MMF: 33157.30 A·turns
    • Number of Turns (I = 2 A): 16579

Notes

  • The MMF calculation assumes the magnetic field is in free space (no steel yoke), leading to high MMF values (e.g., ~33157 A·turns for C-12). If a steel yoke is used (with a permeability ~3000), the MMF would be much lower. Contact the developer to modify the formula if needed.
  • The tool checks inputs to ensure they make sense (e.g., charge can’t exceed atomic number, all numbers must be positive).
  • A warning for magnetic fields above 2 T helps identify designs that may be hard to build.
